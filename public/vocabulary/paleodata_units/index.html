practical salinity unit

Definition

a relative unit for the salinity of water that is defined in terms of the ratio of the electrical conductivity of a water sample at 15 degrees Celsius and the pressure of one standard atmosphere, to that of a solution of 32.4356 grams of potassium chloride (KCl) in 1 kilogram of water at the same temperature and pressure. By definition, a ratio that is exactly equal to one corresponds to a practical salinity of 35.
